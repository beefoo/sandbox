Note
Cataloged from accompanying paperwork.

Unpublished recording consisting of roughly edited segments relating to language, song, and musical and cultural song and performance styles.

Duration estimated.

The first segment is a version of the Tony Schwartz program "Learning a Language," 1172619 / RXF 3637.

Technical note (Matt):
This tape has paper leader at the head and paper and blank tape leader at the tail. The segments are all separated by paper leader.

Content note:
"1. Learning a Language.  Reenah's father tells of the difficulties encountered. 4:30  2. Reenah's Father (in Polish) 'Don't pepper Peter the hog with pepper because you over-pepper Peter the hog with pepper.' 0:26  3. People and language: a) a child stumbling over words, b) a man with a Southern accent, c) a foreigner who says that doors are closed to him when he talks. 0:28  4. African Song 'Senzenena.'  Mrs. Frieda Matthews discusses its history; the song (a one word song) means 'What have we done (to deserve this)?' With group and violin accompaniment. 5:56  5. Group and violin accompaniment of 'Senzenena.' 0:42  6. Mrs. Frieda Matthews on Africa: its languages, the religious fervor of the campaign, the singing of 'Senzenena.' 2:12  7. Brief introduction and an American group singing 'Senzenena' (w/ violin accompaniment). 2:15  8. Leadbelly and his 12 string guitar; George Gershwin at the piano; Leadbelly at the piano; the similarity between the two is noted. 3:14  9. Leadbelly; George Gershwin; (no narration). 3:30  10. Penny whistle with Herb Levy; multiple recording; Harry Belafonte and group. 1:01  11. Herb Levy and the penny whistle; introductory remarks. 1:01  12. Herb Levy, Harry Belafonte & Company. 3:30  13. Kathleen Walsh telling a little about (and singing a sample of) Irish mouth music. 3:56  14. Hasidic Tunes"--Accompanying collection documentation.
